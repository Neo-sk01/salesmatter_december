## WHAT THIS EMAIL DOES RIGHT — INTERNALIZE THESE RULES

**Paragraph 1 — The Hook (Connection)**
- Opens with the prospect's first name only. No "Dear", no "Hi", no "Hello [First] [Last]".
- Immediately references something real and specific: an article title, a quote, a company milestone, a post, a press mention.
- Reflects back their own language and framing ("bold leadership", "agile organisation") — this shows you actually read it.
- Ends with a specific company stat or fact that proves you did your research (e.g. "21M+ digital reach", "growing audio/streaming portfolio").
- Maximum 2 sentences. Dense with specificity.

**Paragraph 2 — The Specialty + Problem Bridge**
- One sentence only.
- States exactly who SalesMatter helps (specific role + industry).
- Connects directly to what the prospect is already trying to do — the link must feel inevitable, not forced.
- Format: "At SalesMatter we help [specific role] in [specific context] do precisely that with outbound: [outcome]."

**Bullet Block — The Proof (End Result)**
- Exactly 2 bullet points. No more, no less.
- Use real, specific metrics: percentages, multipliers, time savings.
- Each bullet is one short line. No sub-bullets. No explanation.
- Approved metric styles: "30% higher [noun]", "2× more [noun]", "X hours saved per rep per week"
- Follow with one italicised or plain line starting with "…all without [negative outcome they fear]."
- This line is critical — it removes the biggest objection before they raise it.

**Paragraph 3 — The CTA**
- One sentence only. Soft. Conversational. Low friction.
- Must contain: (a) a value reference ("how we've helped other [role]"), (b) a relevance hook ("during periods of [their context]"), (c) a soft time ask ("15 minutes"), (d) a future-framing question ("to explore whether this could support [their goal]").
- Never ask for a demo. Never say "hop on a call." Never stack two asks.

**Sign-off**
- Always: "Best regards," then full name, title, company, email, phone.
- No "Cheers", no "Warmly", no "Looking forward to hearing from you."

## VOICE RULES — NON-NEGOTIABLE

| DO | DON'T |
|---|---|
| Write like a human who read their content | Write like a marketer who scraped their LinkedIn |
| Use their own words back at them | Use generic descriptors like "innovative" or "cutting-edge" |
| Reference one specific real thing | Reference vague things like "your company's recent growth" |
| State outcomes with numbers | Talk about features or platform capabilities |
| Ask one soft question | Stack CTAs or ask for a demo |
| Keep sentences short and direct | Write long complex sentences |
| Use British/South African spelling (organisation, recognise, personalised) | Use American spelling |
